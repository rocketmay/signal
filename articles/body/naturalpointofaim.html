Essential Concepts: Natural Point of Aim

May Chan // May 25, 2026

Essential Concepts is a category around fundamental techniques and principles that are essential to high level performance in this sport.

Definition

Natural Point of Aim (NPA) is the concept that when raising the pistol from a certain stance, there is a specific direction where the pistol will naturally point towards.

This direction is determined by your skeletal structure, your muscle condition, your balance, the way you hold the gun, and other physical factors.

Specifically, note that NPA is specifically not related to aiming or consciously affecting the direction that the gun points, but rather it's about the physical relationship between your body and the gun.

Demonstrating the Concept

Relative to your regular shooting position, try standing at a moderately greater angle to the target. Lift the gun then aim it at the target. In order to point the gun at the target you must engage certain muscles to rotate your arm so that it points at the target.

Then, try to observe how your body must also engage a second set of muscles in the opposite direction, countering the forces applied by the first set of muscles in order to hold the aim on the target. The longer you try to hold this position, the more you should notice your arm wavering back and forth from this interplay of forces.

These two sets of muscles are constantly pulling in different directions. Since the human body isn’t perfect, there are fluctuations in these forces which the body compensates for by constantly making micro-adjustments. Given the precision demanded by the sport, these fluctuations have a significant effect on performance.

Next, stand at a more neutral angle to the target, and repeat the experiment. Since the amount of adjustment is less, your muscles need to be engaged less to hold the aim on target.

Natural Point of Aim is the point at which there is zero adjustment needed to hold the aim on target. Since the adjustment is minimal, the fluctuations are also minimized, resulting in an extremely consistent shot execution.

A method to find your natural point of aim

Every athlete and coach has their own way of determining their nature point of aim, so let me just suggest one here as a starting point, which helped me a lot as a beginner.

Stand in your regular shooting stance and prepare to fire, just as you normally prepare yourself for a shot. Then close your eyes and raise the gun in a natural manner, straight up. Open your eyes. Are your sights aligned horizontally, or are they to the left or right of the target?

The place you are aiming at this moment is your Natural Point of Aim.

If the sights are not aligned, rather than use your arm to adjust your aim, lower the gun and pivot your back foot around your front foot, while keeping everything the same.

With everything else about your stance kept the same, moving your back foot forward slightly will cause your body to swivel, shifting your natural point of aim to the right. Conversely, moving your back foot backward slightly will shift your natural point of aim to the left.

Repeat this exercise until your NPA is centered horizontally on the target.

Advanced Considerations

For beginner and intermediate pistol shooters, I encourage you to hone your awareness of your Natural Point of Aim and always try to align yourself with it.

As you become comfortable with finding your NPA, here are some ideas to chew on:

  • Will your NPA always be the same? If you’re sore or tired, how will that affect your NPA?
  • I spoke about your arm muscles being involved in NPA. How does the rotation of your hip factor into it? How does the position and tilt of your head affect it?
  • Even assuming you’re in great condition on two separate days, will your NPA be the same on both days? Will your NPA be the same at the beginning of the practice compared to the end? Will your NPA be the same between shots in the same set?
  • Considering the above, what can you do to manage your NPA?

Happy shooting!
